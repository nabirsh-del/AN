Meet the Rancher

John & Sarah Smith

The Smith Family Ranch has been raising cattle in West Texas for over 35 years. What started with John's grandfather on 400 acres has grown into a fourth-generation operation spanning 2,400 acres of Texas grassland.

John grew up learning ranching from his father, who learned from his father before him. After studying agriculture at Texas Tech, John returned home to continue the family tradition. His wife Sarah, a former agricultural extension agent, brought modern sustainable practices to the operation.

"We raise cattle the right way," John explains. "Our animals spend their entire lives on pasture, eating grass like they're meant to. No feedlots, no growth hormones, no shortcuts. Just good land, clean water, and time."

"When people buy our beef, they're not just getting meat. They're getting a piece of our family's legacy and our commitment to doing right by the land and the animals." ‚Äî John Smith, Owner

How We Raise Our Cattle

üå±

100% Grass-Fed

Our cattle graze on native Texas grasses their entire lives. No grain finishing, no feedlots. We rotate pastures to maintain healthy grassland and give the land time to recover.

üö´

No Hormones or Antibiotics

We never use growth hormones or routine antibiotics. Our cattle stay healthy through good genetics, proper nutrition, and low-stress handling. If an animal needs medical treatment, it's removed from the program.

üíß

Natural Water Sources

Our cattle drink from natural stock tanks fed by rainwater and groundwater wells. Clean water is essential to animal health and meat quality.

üîÑ

Rotational Grazing

We practice regenerative grazing, moving cattle between pastures to prevent overgrazing and promote soil health. This builds carbon in the soil and improves the land year after year.

üëÅÔ∏è

Low-Stress Handling

Calm animals produce better meat. We use quiet handling techniques, never rushing or stressing the herd. Our cattle are accustomed to human presence and remain calm throughout their lives.

‚úÖ

USDA Inspected

All processing is done at USDA-inspected facilities that meet federal food safety standards. Every cut is traceable back to our ranch and the specific animal.

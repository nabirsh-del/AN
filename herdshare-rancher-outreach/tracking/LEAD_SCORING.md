# HerdShare ‚Äî Lead Scoring Framework

## Purpose

This framework provides a standardized method to score and prioritize rancher leads from 1 (lowest priority) to 5 (highest priority).

**Goal:** Focus outreach time on the ranchers most likely to become long-term HerdShare supply partners.

---

## Scoring Dimensions

Each lead is scored across **5 dimensions**. The final score is the **average** of all five.

```
Final Score = (Throughput + Proximity + Sales Channel + Operation Type + Responsiveness) / 5
```

Round to nearest 0.5 (e.g., 3.2 ‚Üí 3.0, 3.8 ‚Üí 4.0)

---

## Dimension 1: Estimated Throughput (Annual Head Count)

**What we're measuring:** Volume potential and operational scale.

| Score | Annual Head Count | Rationale |
|-------|------------------|-----------|
| **5** | 200+ head | High volume, strong commercial operation |
| **4** | 100-199 head | Solid mid-sized operation |
| **3** | 50-99 head | Viable commercial scale |
| **2** | 20-49 head | Small but potentially growing |
| **1** | <20 head | Hobby scale, low priority |

**How to estimate:**
- Auction records (lot sizes √ó frequency)
- Herd size mentioned in association directories
- Direct ask during initial conversation
- County extension agent intel

**Notes:**
- A rancher selling 50 head today may scale to 150 next year‚Äîdon't disqualify small operations entirely
- Focus on trajectory, not just current volume

---

## Dimension 2: Proximity to HerdShare Hub

**What we're measuring:** Logistical feasibility and freight cost efficiency.

| Score | Distance from Hub/Processor | Rationale |
|-------|----------------------------|-----------|
| **5** | 0-60 miles | Ideal freight economics |
| **4** | 61-100 miles | Strong logistical fit |
| **3** | 101-150 miles | Acceptable, manageable costs |
| **2** | 151-200 miles | Marginal, only if high volume |
| **1** | 200+ miles | Logistically challenging |

**How to measure:**
- Use Google Maps or routing software (ranch address ‚Üí hub address)
- Consider road quality and accessibility (paved vs. gravel)
- Account for seasonal access issues (flood zones, mountain passes)

**Notes:**
- A 150-mile rancher selling 200 head may be better than a 50-mile rancher selling 30 head
- Proximity matters most when volumes are similar

---

## Dimension 3: Current Sales Channel

**What we're measuring:** Openness to alternative buyers and contract flexibility.

| Score | Current Sales Channel | Rationale |
|-------|----------------------|-----------|
| **5** | Spot auction (no contracts) | Highest flexibility, seeking alternatives |
| **4** | Local buyer (informal relationship) | Open to diversification |
| **3** | Mixed (auction + some contracts) | Willing to split allocations |
| **2** | Mostly contracted (1-2 buyers) | Limited availability |
| **1** | Fully contracted/exclusive | Not available (respect existing commitments) |

**How to assess:**
- Ask directly: "How do you currently sell your cattle?"
- Observe auction participation frequency
- Listen for frustration with current channels (pricing volatility, buyer reliability)

**Notes:**
- Ranchers frustrated with auctions are ideal prospects
- Do NOT poach ranchers from existing contracts‚Äîit's unethical and damages reputation

---

## Dimension 4: Operation Type & Independence

**What we're measuring:** Fit with HerdShare's independent rancher focus.

| Score | Operation Type | Rationale |
|-------|---------------|-----------|
| **5** | Independent family ranch (owner-operator) | Perfect ICP fit |
| **4** | Independent LLC/partnership (small group) | Good fit, aligned incentives |
| **3** | Ranch manager (reports to absentee owner) | Possible, but decision-making may be slower |
| **2** | Part of small regional cooperative | Mixed‚Äîmay have other obligations |
| **1** | Corporate subsidiary / vertically integrated | Not target profile |

**How to assess:**
- Review business structure (listed on websites, association directories)
- Ask: "Tell me about your operation‚Äîare you owner-operated?"
- Look for red flags: "Regional Director" titles, corporate parent companies

**Notes:**
- Owner-operators have decision-making authority and aligned incentives
- Corporate operations often have procurement teams and rigid processes (not ideal fit)

---

## Dimension 5: Responsiveness & Engagement

**What we're measuring:** Likelihood of conversion based on initial interactions.

| Score | Responsiveness | Rationale |
|-------|---------------|-----------|
| **5** | Answers phone, asks questions, expresses interest | Ready to engage |
| **4** | Returns calls within 24-48 hours, open to conversation | Interested but busy |
| **3** | Slow to respond but eventually engages | Cautious, needs nurturing |
| **2** | Minimal engagement, vague interest | Low priority follow-up |
| **1** | No response after 3+ attempts | Deprioritize (revisit in 6 months) |

**How to assess:**
- Track initial outreach response time
- Note quality of conversation (engaged vs. distracted)
- Observe question-asking (curious ranchers = higher intent)

**Notes:**
- Responsiveness often correlates with openness to change
- Non-responsive ranchers aren't necessarily uninterested‚Äîtiming matters
- Re-score after 2-3 months if situation changes

## Dynamic Re-Scoring

Scores are **not static**. Re-score leads when:

### Positive Signals (Increase Score)
- Rancher reaches out proactively (+1 on Responsiveness)
- Herd size increases (annual review, +1 on Throughput)
- Current buyer relationship ends (Score 2 ‚Üí 5 on Sales Channel)
- New HerdShare hub opens closer to rancher (+1 on Proximity)

### Negative Signals (Decrease Score)
- No response after 3+ follow-ups (-1 on Responsiveness)
- Signs exclusive contract with competitor (-4 on Sales Channel)
- Operation downsizes significantly (-1 on Throughput)

**Review and re-score quarterly** to keep pipeline accurate.

## Common Scoring Mistakes

### ‚ùå Don't:
- Score based on gut feeling without data
- Ignore proximity in favor of volume (logistics matter)
- Assume all auction sellers are the same (assess responsiveness)
- Set scores and never revisit them (rancher situations change)

### ‚úÖ Do:
- Use consistent criteria across all leads
- Gather data before scoring (don't guess head count)
- Re-score when new information emerges
- Focus 80% of time on Tier 1-2 leads

## Success Metrics

Track scoring effectiveness:
- **Conversion rate by tier:** What % of Tier 1 leads onboard? (Target: 30-40%)
- **Time to close by tier:** How long from first contact to onboarding? (Tier 1 should be fastest)
- **Score accuracy:** Do high-scored leads actually convert better? (Adjust criteria if not)
